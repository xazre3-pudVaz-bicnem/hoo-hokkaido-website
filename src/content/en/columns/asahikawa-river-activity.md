Asahikawa is Hokkaido's second city, with Asahiyama Zoo, a serious ramen scene, and a handy position as a base for trips down to Biei and Furano. Here's how to add a few hours on the river to all of that.

## Nature is closer to the city than you think

Say "Asahikawa" and most people picture a city. Go a little way out, though, and you're among rivers and forest. That short distance between urban sightseeing and real nature is one of the best things about this area.

Sightseeing in the morning, on the river in the afternoon: that combination is easy here in a way it isn't in many places.

## The Asahikawa Relax Downriver

HOO runs the [Asahikawa Relax Downriver](/activities/asahikawa-relax-downriver), a relaxed boat trip down a river in the Asahikawa area.

It isn't a trip built around thrills. You ride the current and take in the scenery and the life around the water, and because your guide is with you throughout, it works well as a first river activity.

## Fitting it into your itinerary

- **Check where it sits relative to your base.** We share the meeting point when you book. If your accommodation is already sorted, mention it when you enquire and we'll advise.
- **Combine it with Furano and Biei.** Asahikawa, Biei and Furano line up neatly, and since HOO also runs trips in [Biei](/activities/biei-relax-downriver) and [Furano](/activities/furano-relax-downriver), you can pick the area that fits your route.
- **Don't forget to book ahead.** Every activity requires advance booking, so get in touch as soon as your dates are set.
