With river activities, the questions you ask before booking are what let you relax on the day. Here's a checklist of what to confirm before you book any trip that takes a boat down a river.

## 1. Check what kind of trip it is

Trips that go by the name "rafting" range from thrill-first runs through white water to gentle floats that are all about the scenery.

Before you book, find out **what kind of time you'll actually be spending on the water**. Turn up with the wrong picture in your head and you'll either be underwhelmed or, worse, uneasy.

For the record, HOO's [Relax Downriver](/activities/furano-relax-downriver) is exactly what the name suggests: a trip built around taking the current and the nature around it slowly.

## 2. Check who can take part

- The minimum group size
- Age requirements (whether children or older guests can join)
- Health requirements (who to ask about medical conditions or pregnancy)

These vary by operator and by trip. If any of them might apply to you, contact the operator directly before booking.

## 3. Check the practicalities

- The meeting point and time
- How long it takes, since it shapes the rest of your day
- What to wear and bring, and what equipment is provided
- The price and how to pay

## 4. Check what happens if the trip is cancelled

River trips can be cancelled or changed because of the weather and river conditions.

- When and how you'll be told if a trip is called off
- The conditions and any fee if you cancel yourself

Settle those two at booking time and nothing catches you out at the last minute.

## In short

The type of trip, who can take part, the practicalities, and cancellations. Cover those four and you've done your homework.
